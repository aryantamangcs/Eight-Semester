
## Introduction to Visual Perception

#### 1.1 What is Visual Perception?

Visual Perception is the process by which your brain interprets and makes meaning from what your eyes see.

Your eyes collect light and send signals to the brain.
Now in your brain those signals become object, depth, motion and meaning.

**Visual** -> Your eyes collecting information
**Visual Perception** -> Your brain interpreting and making meaning out of visual information.


#### 1.2 What is Visual System ?

A system where multiple parts work together to convert light into meaningful information is called visual system.

Light -> Eye -> Electrical Signals -> Brain Processing -> Meaningful Information(Perception).

1. Light enters the eye
2. Cornea — the first focusing layer (bend (refract) incoming light and performs most of the eye focusing)
3. Pupil and iris — controlling brightness(After passing the cornea, light reaches the pupil The iris (colored part) controls pupil size.)
4. Lens — fine focus adjustment (change shape to focus at different distances)
5. Retina — converting light into neural signals (this is the most fascinating part of visual system)
6. Optic nerve — transmitting information (signals leaves via this nerve from eye to brain)
