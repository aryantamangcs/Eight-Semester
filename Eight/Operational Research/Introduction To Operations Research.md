## What is Operational Research?

Operation Research is the process of utilizing various tools and techniques from various disciplines(fields) such as mathematics, statistics, economics, psychology, engineering to form a set of knowledge which helps in decision making.

According to Thomas, ***OR is an art of giving bad answers to the problems which otherwise have worse answers***

You basically use different tools from different disciplines and form a new tools which you will use in decision making.

## What happens when you solve problem through the Operational Research?

You will have a rational basis for your decisions instead of just intuition, personal judgement and common sense.

## How to implement it in Real life?

If you want to make good rational decisions in your life, you must take Operational Research into consideration. You have to follow the following framework to help yourself to make logical decisions:

#### 1.1 Step 1. Identify the problem / objective?

The first step before doing anything is to properly understand the problem or what you are trying to achieve. 

The best way to identify any problem is by asking the right questions.

***Note: Don't ask what is the problem? Ask what will happen If I solve this problem?

Don't ask why cannot I manage my time properly. Ask yourself, what your day would look like if you could properly manage your time. Then you will understand the problem with more clarity.


#### 1.2 Step 2. Identify the Constraints

The second step would be to find the constraints beforehand so that you can plan better.

For example In my time management problem. 
My biggest constraint would be I only have 24 hours, I must sleep 8 hours and I must go to the college. Things like that.

Identifying constraints can help you plan better or consider them while creating a model.

#### 1.3 Step 3. List Decision Variables

Decision Variables are something that you can control. Things that are under your administration are called decision variables.

Now in this phase, you have to list what you can control.

For example, You cannot control there is 24 hours in a day, You cannot control college timing, You cannot control the distance between your home and the college premises.

However you can control
- what to study
- where to put your time in
- where not to waster your time into
#### 1.4 Step 4. Build your model

Model is just a simulation of real world.
You try to simulate the real world by using mathematical tools and functions and equations.

For instance, Creating a spreadsheet for my time management is one of the model

#### 1.5 Step 5. Choose Feasible Alternatives

After feeding data to the model, you will find a list of alternatives while making decisions. SO the next step would be to find the most feasible alternative for your case.

#### 1.6 Step 6. Measure outcomes and Repeat

The last step would be to measure and monitor the outcomes and repeat and rearrange the plan. Because not every plan is perfect.

## Limitations of Operational Research

1. OR is highly based on assumptions (only predictions, not concrete answers and facts)
2. Just a simulation cannot model real world.
3. Requires specialized knowledge (Mathematics, statistics, economics, engineering etc)
4. Gap between Manager and OR Specialist
5. Quality of Output depends upon Quality of Input (Garbage In Garbage Out)
